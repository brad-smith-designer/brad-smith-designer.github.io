Currently I'm a Lead Digital Interaction Designer with RBC Royal Bank of Canada.

My time is spent fully integrated with RBC's design and product team, where I perform a split role. On the one hand, I work within agile squads focussed on delivering enhancements, new product features, and 0-1 experiences. One the other hand, I'm the custodian of the design system 'spoke' for our part of the business, looking after a component and pattern library to serve 20+ design staff. The work requires me to balance the needs of design team, the product team, the tech team, and our banking clients whilst also contributing to the overall RBC design language.

My work always values simplicity, usability and accessibility. I question everything, and I like to avoid trends. I ask questions and try to get to the heart of the problem. Most of all I'm open-minded.

I've been designing for over 12 years. My experience is predominently in UI/UX, and I place huge importance on being involved at all stages of the design process, especially research and frontend implementation. I also value time connecting with stakeholders and socialising ideas.

For early ideation I use a pen and paper, and conversation. Alongside sketching I use the likes of Figma and Mural to create alignment between parties, produce rapid high-fidelity prototypes to engage stakeholders, test with users, and iterate quickly.

In the past I practiced more in-depth prototyping by writing HTML and CSS, and occasionally JS where I could. Writing frontend code for design is controversial but beneficial in my opinion. Whilst this isn't part of today's workflow, having that background allows me to think in terms of reusable components, directly influence the accessibility aspects of a project, and speak the same language as frontend developers.
